Publications

01Morgan et al. 2020

Assessment of Outcomes in Rare Subtypes of Breast Cancer

Patients at Ohio State University Comprehensive Cancer Center- James between January 1st 1994 and December 31st 2014 with triple negative breast cancer or metaplastic breast cancer were eligible for the study. Each MBC patient was matched with three stage and age-matched non-metaplastic TNBC controls. Stage (1-4) had to be identical and age at diagnosis had to be within 10 years. Demographics and treatments were compared between MBC and TNBC controls, and multivariate analysis was performed to assess survival outcomes between groups. Observation time was defined as the time from diagnosis to death or censoring. A separate cohort of TNBC patients and corresponding tissue samples was used to assess histopathology comparative to MBC samples, looking specifically at CD8, CD163, and PD-L1 presence.
